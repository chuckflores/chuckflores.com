Project

Umi Kitchen

Umi Kitchen is a service that allows its users to plan their weekly meals by ordering food from home cooks in their neighborhood.

We were tasked with designing the dashboard, meal system, and forms for the Umi home cooks to submit and manage meals.

Dashboard

The dashboard contains everything the Umi home cooks need to add meals to the app and fulfill orders. The Meals page is the center of the dashboard.

Organization

The Orders page contains all essential information for the cooks to fullfill orders. Past orders can be viewed to plan future meal portions and maximize profits.

On Boarding

Filling in all of the important information is crucial for presenting the home cooks on the app. The on boarding process makes it easy for cooks to set up their Umi Kitchens.

Responsive

Half of the cooks interviewed often do their meal planning on their phone while out of the house. Home cooks also need to see how many portions to plan for each meal when shopping for ingredients. This required a responsive design.

UI Elements
Tagging System

Used when adding ingredients to a meal. The tagging system allows for consistent formatting in the Umi Kitchen app.

Progress Bar

Used during the on boarding process for first time cooks.

UI Elements
MINI CALENDAR

When adding a meal the cook must pick a date. The mini calendar shows days that are unavailable (grey zone) and days with meals (orange).

HOVER MODAL

Used to answer questions a cook might have when adding a meal.

Meal System

In order to keep meals organized, a meal system was created to show the various meal states. This gives the cooks multiple options when planning meals.

Live Meal

Meals currently on sale through the app. These meals are un-editable.

Published Meal

Meals that will become live once they enter the live zone.

Draft Meal

Designed for cooks to plan meals with out commiting them the app.

PAst Meal

Resell meals without having to fill out the entire form.

iOS APP HOME SCREEN

While the iOS app for Umi Kitchen was being developed I assisted in tweaks to home feed interface. The design came out of a need to promote the home cook’s kitchens and highlight the food.

Orignal
Cook’s Kitchen

This element was very important in helping the cooks form a brand on Umi Kitchen. This was to form more connections with users and encourage repeat purchases.

Food Image

Users wanted to see to food they were ordering. Discovered through user feedback.

Cook’s Picture

Another way to improve the cook’s brand was to highlight their photo next to the meal. This was very important to home cooks as we discovered in our interviews.
