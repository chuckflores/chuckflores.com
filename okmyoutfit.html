User Journey

We tested the current sign up process with potential users to discover pain points within the exisiting style profile. Throughout the test users were periodically asked how they felt on a scale of 1 (being frustrated) to 10 (feeling happy). User journeys (visualized below) were created to visualize the results from our usability test to the client.

Below are 3 user journeys overlayed on top of one another for effect.

Changing the Service

After user interviews and tests it was discovered that users wanted the stylist to take their measurements at the first visit. This lead to giving the user the option of requesting in-home measurements and style assessment or completing the sign up form and trying on clothes the first visit.

Positive & Engaging Questions

We redesigned the style profile questions to be clear in their language and promote positive body image. Additionally we supplemented the questions with secondary copy. This clarified the question and told the user why the information is needed.
